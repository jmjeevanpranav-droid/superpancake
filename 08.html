Systemic Failures

Regulatory &
Systemic Lapses

The Telgi scam exposed fundamental weaknesses in how stamp paper systems were designed, managed, and monitored by the state.

07

Decentralized Chaos

State-managed supply chains lacked central oversight or standardized protocols, creating exploitable fragmentation.

Outdated Technology

Reliance on obsolete printing tech and simple security features made papers easy to forge with retired government equipment.

Inadequate Tracking

Poor inventory management allowed Telgi to engineer artificial shortages without triggering immediate alarms.

Fragmented Oversight

Responsibility was split across multiple departments and vendors, meaning no single authority held accountability.

Information Silos

Limited data sharing between agencies meant suspicious patterns in one region were not cross-referenced elsewhere.
