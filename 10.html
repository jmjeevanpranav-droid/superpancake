Lessons Learned: Prevention & Reform

Systemic improvements to secure institutions against future fraud

Stronger Controls

Governments and corporations must enforce robust internal audits and surprise checks. Physical security at printing presses now includes mandatory CCTV surveillance and digital access logs to deter insider theft.

Rigorous Audits Preventive Oversight

Modern Technology

The shift to e-Stamping (via SHCIL) replaces vulnerable physical papers with secure digital records. Unique identifiers and digital signatures make modern counterfeiting significantly harder than duplicating old stamp dies.

e-Stamping Digital Security

Inter-Agency Unity

Improved coordination between revenue departments, police, and banks allows for real-time fraud detection. Sharing data on unusual sales spikes or vendor activity helps identify anomalies before they scale.

Intel Sharing Unified Response

Continuous technological upgrades and vigilance are critical to stay ahead of sophisticated fraud.
