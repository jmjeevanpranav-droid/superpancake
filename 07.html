Internal Control Breakdown

Corporate Governance Failures

The scam wasn't just criminal ingenuity; it was enabled by a collapse of checks and balances within the very institutions designed to prevent fraud.

Key Lesson

"Trust but verify" was replaced by "Trust and ignore" due to profit pressures and systemic lethargy.

Procurement Blindness

Banks and insurance firms purchased papers from unauthorized third-party vendors without verifying authenticity with state treasuries.

Weak Internal Controls

Complete lack of periodic audits, stock reconciliation, or "mystery shopper" checks to validate the legitimacy of sensitive documents.

Due Diligence Gaps

Telgi employed "management executives" to sell forgeries. Companies failed to vet these intermediaries or question their unauthorized bulk discounts.

Risk Management Void

No red-flag triggers for anomalies. Massive spikes in stamp paper purchases during an "official shortage" went unquestioned.

Incentive Misalignment

Operational speed and cost-saving (via discounts) were prioritized over compliance and ethical sourcing.
